Critical Listening Assistant

Critical Listening Assistant explores how software can help people listen more actively in meetings, lectures, debates, and media environments without overwhelming them.

Primary question

How can software support careful listening while preserving the listener's own judgment?

Likely use

Support reflection and careful review in contexts where attention and interpretation matter.

Overview

Listening as an active intellectual skill.

The project is oriented around listening as a form of reasoning. The concept suggests a workspace for staying attentive, reflective, and intellectually responsible in complex conversations.

This project fits especially well with broader work on critical thinking, media literacy, and intellectual responsibility.

Focus areas

Potential strengths

  • Careful listening support
  • Structured reflection after listening
  • Reflective review of ideas and arguments
  • Use in classrooms, meetings, and public discourse
