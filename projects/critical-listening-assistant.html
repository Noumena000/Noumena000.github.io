Critical Listening Assistant

Critical Listening Assistant explores how technology might help people listen more carefully without replacing their judgment. This browser demonstration uses a fictional faculty discussion to reveal claims, reasons, evidence, assumptions, objections, and questions.

Design principle

Listening as an active intellectual practice.

The project treats listening as more than transcription. It asks what is being claimed, what supports the claim, what remains assumed, what objections matter, and what questions would move the conversation forward.

The goal is not to automate judgment. It is to make the structure of a discussion easier to examine.

Reasoning categories

What the assistant surfaces

  • Claims and proposals
  • Reasons and evidence
  • Unstated assumptions
  • Objections and counterexamples
  • Clarifying and evaluative questions
  • Revisions and unresolved issues

Privacy and ethical use

Designed to support judgment—not surveillance.

01

Consent before capture

Any native implementation should make recording visible and require appropriate participant consent.

02

Local-first processing

The development direction prioritizes on-device analysis and user control over storage, deletion, and sharing.

03

Interpretation, not authority

Categories and questions are prompts for reflection. They are not definitive judgments about speakers or intentions.

04

No covert participation

The project is not intended for hidden monitoring, employee surveillance, or undisclosed analysis of private conversations.

About this browser demonstration

It uses only fictional text stored in the webpage. It does not request microphone permission, record audio, send content to an AI provider, or save visitor data.

About the native prototype

The separate macOS prototype explores transcription and local reasoning support. Its implementation remains private while consent, privacy, and reliability safeguards are developed.
