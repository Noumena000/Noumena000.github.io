Critical Listening Assistant

Critical Listening Assistant explores how software can help people listen more actively in meetings, lectures, debates, and media environments by surfacing structure rather than drowning the listener in more noise.

Primary question

How can AI help people track claims, assumptions, and reasoning patterns while preserving the listener's own judgment?

Likely use

Support note-taking, argument tracking, and reflective review in contexts where attention and interpretation matter.

Overview

Listening as an active intellectual skill.

The project is oriented around listening as a form of reasoning. Instead of treating audio input merely as transcription material, the concept suggests a workspace for following ideas, distinguishing claims from evidence, and revisiting what matters most.

This project fits especially well with broader work on critical thinking, media literacy, and intellectual responsibility.

Focus areas

Potential strengths

  • Real-time reasoning support
  • Structured reflection after listening
  • Claim and evidence awareness
  • Use in classrooms, meetings, and public discourse
