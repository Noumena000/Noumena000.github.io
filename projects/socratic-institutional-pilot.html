Socratic Institutional Pilot

The Socratic Institutional Pilot is an institution-facing public demonstration of how learning objectives, knowledge packs, structured dialogue, evidence portfolios, approved AI providers, and faculty judgment can operate as one governed educational workflow.

Interactive Institutional Demo
A fictional public pilot for architecture review, educational discussion, and responsible implementation planning.

Purpose

To show how institutions can govern AI-supported learning through curriculum alignment, transparent evidence requirements, provider boundaries, and faculty review.

Institution-facing demonstration The public pilot follows one fictional applied-ethics module from learning objectives and knowledge-pack configuration through Socratic dialogue, evidence review, and provider governance.

Central thesis

The product is the educational decision system surrounding the model.

A language model can generate and interpret language, but an institution also needs stable learning objectives, evidence requirements, pedagogical policies, provenance, privacy boundaries, and accountable human judgment. The pilot demonstrates an educational-intelligence layer that gives an approved AI provider a bounded instructional role.

Curriculum governs the dialogue; the model does not independently define what counts as learning.

Institutional priorities

What the pilot emphasizes

  • Curriculum-defined objectives and evidence requirements
  • Traceable pedagogical decisions
  • Replaceable, institution-approved AI providers
  • Transcript-linked evidence for faculty review
  • Human authority over evaluation and implementation

Demonstrated workflow

From curriculum to faculty-reviewable evidence.

The interactive pilot guides visitors through a module overview, a faculty-authored knowledge pack, a learner launch, structured Socratic dialogue, an educational decision trace, a faculty evidence portfolio, and the boundary between the institutional platform and an external AI provider.

  • Learning objectives define what understanding should look like.
  • The knowledge pack specifies concepts, misconceptions, evidence, and stopping conditions.
  • The educational layer identifies missing evidence and selects the next instructional move.
  • The provider performs bounded language tasks.
  • Faculty receive evidence for review rather than an automatic grade.

Research ecosystem

Institutional implementation of a broader educational-AI program.

The Socratic Reasoning Engine explores the underlying dialogue model. The Socratic Assessment Platform demonstrates learner understanding through questioning. The Critical Listening Assistant supports interpretation of discussions and arguments. The Institutional Pilot shows how these ideas can be situated inside a curriculum-governed, faculty-centered system.

Demonstration boundary

What remains protected

  • Private reasoning-engine implementation
  • Production prompts and orchestration
  • Institutional credentials and integrations
  • Learner records and real course content
  • Deployment, security, and provider configuration

Portfolio notice

An independent fictional demonstration.

The project is independently developed for research, professional portfolio, and institutional-discussion purposes. It does not represent an official deployment by an employer or educational institution, and it does not reproduce private institutional course content or learner information.
